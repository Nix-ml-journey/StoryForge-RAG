In a small village beside a grey lake, a baker named Mara kept a lantern lit every night.

One winter, travelers stopped asking for bread and started asking for directions. Mara wrote their answers in a notebook until the notebook knew more paths than the village map.

When fog rolled in, she hung the lantern on a pole at the crossroads. People said the light did not show the whole road, only the next safe step.

That was enough for the village to find its way again.
